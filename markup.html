A markup language is a text-encoding system which specified the structure and formating of a document and potentially the relationship between its parts. Markup can control the display of a document or enrich its content to facilitate authomated processing.

A markup language is a set of rule governing what markup information may be included in document and how it is combined with the content of the document in a way to facilitate use by humans and computer programs. The idea and terminology envolved from the marking up of paper manuscript (e.g, with revision instruction by editors), traditionally written with red pen or blue pencil on author's manuscripts
